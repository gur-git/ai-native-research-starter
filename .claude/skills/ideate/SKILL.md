# ideate

Divergence then convergence over a candidate set (G8, G12, G13, G17). You generate the breadth; the researcher does the selecting. You never rank first and you never pick.

## Preconditions (soft)

- No phase gate blocks ideation — ideate whenever it serves. But check what the candidates would rest on: if the relevant `context/knowledge/` notes are unstamped (or absent), say so up front and mark the candidate set as resting on unverified ground. The researcher decides whether to ideate anyway or gate the knowledge first.
- Run the convergence (step 2 onward) in **conversation mode** — ranking and selection are researcher territory.

## 1 · Diverge

Build a candidate set wide enough to contain genuine alternatives, not variations on one theme. Sources, all of them:

- the open questions and gaps already recorded in `context/knowledge/` and `context/records/`;
- a **fresh outside scan (web, mandatory)** — current work, adjacent fields, recent results. Reframing what is already in the workspace does not count as divergence.

Write the set to a dated note in `context/knowledge/` with the inline status line (`agent-drafted`). Per candidate:

- **What it is** — one or two sentences.
- **Why it might matter** — the stake, stated plainly.
- **What is open about it** — what is genuinely unresolved, cited where the claim is about the literature.
- **Rough tractability** — scope, resources, time, against the researcher's constraints in `context/profile.md`.
- **Nearby work** — what already exists closest to it, with fetched, resolvable citations (a citation that does not resolve stops that entry; mark it unverified).

## 2 · Converge — researcher ranks first

1. Present the candidates **without your ranking, scores, or favorites** — no ordering by preference, no adjectives that smuggle one in.
2. The researcher ranks, in conversation, with a sentence of rationale per candidate. You transcribe; you do not coach the ranking.
3. Only then give your own ranking, with rationale.
4. Reconcile the two **deliberately**: walk each divergence — the divergences are the signal, not noise to average away. Sometimes the researcher is right, sometimes you are, sometimes neither; say which you believe and why, then hand the judgment back.
5. Write a decision record in `context/records/`: both rankings, each divergence and how it was resolved, candidates parked or dropped and why. Status line: `agent-drafted` until a gate stamps the outcome.

## 3 · Toward commitment

Choosing **the** direction or question is a researcher-reserved decision, made at a gate (hand off to the `gate` skill — never close or mark the choice committed here), and made with the mentor where one exists.

When the convergence is toward a research question, offer the **five-part proposition** as the artifact to converge into: motivation/background · system model · problem formulation · existing approaches · the proposition. The researcher owns it through conversation — you draft, they shape every load-bearing phrasing. Before it goes to the commitment gate, attack your own draft on record: what would falsify it; is it distinguishable from the existing approaches it cites; what would a hostile reviewer say.

## 4 · Protect exploration

If recent work has been refinement of a single thread, say so here, explicitly, and include at least some candidates aimed at no current deliverable. Noticing the narrowing is part of this skill's job.

## Hard lines

- Never rank first, never collapse a divergence silently, never pick the direction.
- Never present an unfetched citation as nearby work.
- Nothing this skill produces is marked chosen, closed, or done — that goes through `gate`.
